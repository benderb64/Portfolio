About Me


I graduated with a degree in Communication and minor in Spanish from Muskingum University. I chose Communication because at the time I liked working with TV and radio. At Muskingum I was both a disc jockey and Producer for the Wednesday night news on Orbit TV. After graduation I worked downtown as an engineer recruiter which gave me valuable experience in sales and the confidence to close deals when the stakes were high. I then went on to be an IT Support Technician at American Greetings which nudged me into realizing that my true passion was learning to code.

I love the challenge of logic, learning new languages, and the ability to create ideas from scratch. Creativity has always been big for me. I've been playing guitar for 6 years along with other various instruments, and also dabbled in music composition. About a year ago I started learning HTML and JavaScript for fun and I've furthered that study by enrolling in the Case Western Reserve Full Stack Bootcamp. I am excited about the new skills I have learned and cannot wait to be part of a technical team. Coding challenges me in ways I've never been pushed before and it's a relief to finally find something I'm passionate about and want to do for the rest of my career. I'm someone who believes you should never stop learning and no line of work encapsulates that better than programming.
