About Me


I graduated with a degree in Communication and minor in Spanish from Muskingum University. I chose Communication because I was heavily involved with the school's TV and radio departments. I was even promoted to lead TV Producer for the weekly news on Orbit TV, and given my own radio show. After graduation I worked downtown as an engineer recruiter. This highly improved my sales ability and gave me the confidence to close deals when the stakes were high. I then went on to be an IT Support Technician at American Greetings, but I wanted a job in technology that was more technical.

I soon discovered I love the challenge of programming, learning new languages, and the ability to create ideas from scratch. Creativity has always been important for me. I've been playing guitar for 6 years and play a little drums and harmonica as well. However these days my main drive is on Software Development. In 2018 I started teaching myself HTML and JavaScript the same way I taught myself music. Through YouTube videos. I then furthered that study by enrolling in the Case Western Reserve Full Stack Academy. I am excited about the new skills I have learned and cannot wait to be part of a technical team. Coding challenges me in ways I've never been pushed before and it's a relief to finally find something I'm passionate about and want to do for the rest of my career. I'm someone who believes you should never stop learning and no line of work encapsulates that better than Software Development.
